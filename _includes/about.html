About

I am a PhD student in Robotics at the University of Michigan, supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. My research focuses on force and tactile sensing, actuator design for biomimetic robots, and multimodal sensing (torque, visuotactile, and proprioception) for safe and adaptive robot interaction.

I received my B.S. in Biomedical Engineering with a minor in Mathematics from Georgia Tech, where I worked on medical robotics and actuation design. I also conducted summer research in the CHARM Lab at Stanford University, focusing on hardware design for soft robotic systems.

My long-term research vision is to create sensor-rich, backdrivable robotic systems that enable safety, adaptability, and performance while extending robot capabilities in unstructured environments.
